# User-Ear Seed Cases v0

Status: human-calibration notes for MSSL listening-language work.  
Scope: song-first listening cases, comment-alignment examples, and transfer guards.  
Non-scope: this is not a label schema, not a universal taxonomy, not a final listening report, and not raw comment storage.

## Why this document exists

MSSL should not learn a fixed set of emotional templates from music comments. It should learn a lighter diagnostic move:

```text
Listen to the song first.
Then ask whether a comment actually connects back to that song.
Transfer the question, not the answer.
```

This document stores six human-calibrated cases. Each case includes:

1. A song-first listening profile.
2. Lyric / music / public-review-derived axes.
3. Processed comment-alignment patterns.
4. Processed user-ear listening notes.
5. Non-transferable details.
6. A portable diagnostic question.

The original comments are intentionally not stored. They may contain private memory, platform noise, usernames, dates, and source-specific expression. Only paraphrased patterns and derived judgment rules are kept.

## Global guardrails

- Do not generalize these six songs into universal categories.
- Do not use a seed case as a report template for future songs.
- Do not treat comments as song truth.
- Do not store raw comment text in this repository.
- Do not treat meme, memory, or emotion as automatically useful or automatically useless.
- A useful comment does not have to reconstruct sound directly. It must reconstruct a listening field.
- Every future song must receive its own song-first profile before comment alignment is judged.

## Core mechanism: comment-to-song alignment

For each comment or review, ask four light questions:

```text
Entry: where does the comment enter the song?
Coupling: how does it connect back to this song's lyric, sound, body, scene, memory, culture, or public use?
Scope: what can it support: song structure, public reception, user context, or a report-language hint?
Failure: does it detach into platform behavior, fandom, generic emotion, private story, or empty meme?
```

This is an audit mechanism, not a generator.

## Case 1: 七里香 / Jay Chou

### Song-first listening profile

`七里香` is not a generic youth-memory template. It is a bright summer love-song field built from natural images, handwritten lyric imagery, light rhythmic motion, and a soft, memory-friendly vocal presence. Public reference material describes the song as a soft-rock number with R&B and traditional Chinese instrumentation elements, and notes its inspiration from Xi Murong's poem title *Seven Miles of Fragrance*.

Source lead: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Jasmine_Orange_%28song%29>

### Lyric axis

The lyric axis is a summer love poem: weather, plants, paper, taste, rain, and writing turn affection into a scene. Its youth-memory force does not come from the abstract word `青春`; it comes from concrete sensory objects that make young love feel recoverable.

### Music / arrangement axis

The music is light enough to let memory enter. It does not force tragedy. It lets the listener walk into a sunlit field, a classroom page, a shared headphone, or a remembered summer without needing a heavy dramatic frame.

### Public / review-derived axis

Professional and public descriptions often stress its poetic love-song quality, Chinese-style imagery, and summer-romance feeling. These are useful as context only. They do not authorize treating every youth-related song as a `七里香`-style summer-first-love case.

### Processed positive comment patterns

- A useful comment reconnects the song to shared listening, cassette-era youth, school roads, and the sudden return of a first-love memory.
- A useful comment turns the song's summer imagery into a remembered scene of weather, paper, rain, sweetness, and lost youth.
- A useful comment places the song in a campus night scene where anonymous listeners gather around a voice, making music a temporary community.

### Processed rejected comment patterns

- Generic praise of Jay Chou or belated discovery of his music does not connect back to this song.
- Platform behavior, payment, membership, or access complaints are not listening evidence.
- Listener-identity jokes such as who listens to Jay Chou are fandom noise unless they reconnect to the song's listening field.

### Processed user-ear notes

- The song behaves like a montage of summer youth scenes.
- Its memory field can return listeners to childhood, school years, and a time when ordinary reality still felt pure.
- The lyric may name summer, but the melody and atmosphere make young, serious affection feel three-dimensional.
- Small sound images can be heard as pages, wind, schoolwork, and memory fragments flying past.
- The song preserves not the success of first love, but the remembered sincerity of having once believed in it.

### Final user judgment

`青春的夏日，热烈单纯的小情诗。`

### Non-transferable details

Do not transfer cassette, half-earphone listening, wind, paper, school nights, rain, strawberry, or first love to every youth song.

### Portable diagnostic question

What concrete sensory objects does this song use to preserve youth?

## Case 2: 暗涌 / Faye Wong

### Song-first listening profile

`暗涌` should not be reduced to `love but cannot touch`. The song first builds a grey, suspended, undercurrent-like listening space through piano, melodic suspension, and voice-arrangement motion. The lyric then gives that space a relationship name. Public reference material confirms Chen Huiyang as composer / arranger and Lin Xi as lyricist, and notes the title's `undercurrent` meaning.

Source lead: <https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9A%97%E6%B9%A7>

### Lyric axis

The lyric axis is unspoken love, fear of repeating tragedy, and the impulse to touch what feels beautiful but unreachable. It supports the emotional frame, but it should not override the music's grey spatial field.

### Music / arrangement axis

The key music issue is `melody-arrangement coupling`: voice, piano, and flowing accompaniment create a suspended body. This case is useful for distinguishing lyric-theme similarity from melodic-syntactic or arrangement-coupling similarity.

### Public / review-derived axis

Public descriptions around the song often emphasize the grey mood, piano, undercurrent imagery, and the relation between title and music. These should be used as listening context, not as proof that all unspoken-love songs sound like `暗涌`.

### Processed positive comment patterns

- A useful comment links the song to ambiguity, hesitation, and love that is present but cannot be safely spoken.
- A useful comment paraphrases the emotional posture as wanting to touch and withdrawing the hand.
- A useful comment connects the piano / melody field to coldness, grey sky, rolling cloud, and sinking motion.
- A useful comment hears the original version's piano and voice as staggered, cold, and tightening rather than only reading the lyric.

### Processed rejected comment patterns

- Discovering Faye Wong late says little about this song.
- Saying only that the lyric is `poisonous` is too generic.
- Payment or platform access is not listening evidence.

### Processed user-ear notes

- The song has a strong resemblance to `一丝不挂` at the level of melodic syntax and voice-arrangement coupling, not simply emotional topic.
- Many listeners enter a grey spatial field through melody, then describe the result as ambiguity, secret love, or unrequited desire through lyrics.
- Some love is imagined as destined loss before it is acted on.
- Much of the comment field can become self-moving one-person drama; this must be separated from the song's own structural undercurrent.
- The song can jolt and withdraw like the inner surge of a secret crush.

### Final user judgment

`关于爱未言说，暗自的独角戏。`

### Non-transferable details

Do not transfer grey sky, unreachable touch, piano coldness, or one-person secret love to every song about ambiguity or unspoken feeling.

### Portable diagnostic question

Do the lyric emotion and the music-arrangement space point to the same feeling, or is the comment projecting one onto the other?

## Case 3: 平凡之路 / Pu Shu

### Song-first listening profile

`平凡之路` is not generic inspiration. It is a road song about losing specialness and still continuing. Public reference material identifies it as Pu Shu's song for the film *The Continent* and notes its Golden Horse recognition as an original film song.

Source lead: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pu_Shu>

### Lyric axis

The lyric axis is crossing, losing, wandering, ordinary life, and return. The song turns `road` into a way to accept that life may not grant the promised exceptional destiny.

### Music / arrangement axis

The song's motion is open-road, plain-spoken, and accumulative rather than heroic. Its power lies in allowing ordinary life to remain dignified without becoming triumphalist.

### Public / review-derived axis

Public context around the film theme and the song's broad circulation can support its road / life-path reading. That public context does not mean every personal hardship story is aligned with the song.

### Processed positive comment patterns

- A useful comment links the song to real travel, remote roads, freedom, and the urge to set out again.
- A useful comment transforms a shameful ordinary childhood scene into a remembered road that could have held beauty.
- A useful comment connects academic failure, not being chosen, distance from a dream, and continuing one's own path.

### Processed rejected comment patterns

- A severe private tragedy is not useful if it does not reconnect to the song's road, ordinariness, departure, acceptance, or continuation.
- Quoting the lyric and saying `resonance` is too thin without a concrete listening or life-path connection.
- Turning a life-path song into a fan-love or romance projection can distort the song's axis.

### Processed user-ear notes

- Ordinary lives can still hold vast inner weather.
- People may differ like leaves in a forest rather than heroes in a destiny contest.
- Refusing to degrade plain life into failure is part of the song's moral movement.
- The song supports living for oneself rather than for external ranking.
- Pain does not cancel bloom; a plain person can still live vividly.

### Final user judgment

`平凡不是平庸，平淡的人生依然精彩。`

### Non-transferable details

Do not transfer road trips, Tibet routes, exams, northwestern travel, or academic failure to every life-path song.

### Portable diagnostic question

Does the private story connect to the song's core action: continuing after ordinary failure or disillusion?

## Case 4: 杀死那个石家庄人 / Omnipotent Youth Society

### Song-first listening profile

`杀死那个石家庄人` is not just urban depression. It is a city, family, and generation-pressure song that turns social ruin into rock resistance. Public reference material describes the song as portraying a family left behind by historical change, and notes its strong place in Chinese independent rock culture.

Source leads:  
<https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9D%80%E6%AD%BB%E9%82%A3%E4%B8%AA%E7%9F%B3%E5%AE%B6%E5%BA%84%E4%BA%BA>  
<https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E4%B8%87%E8%83%BD%E9%9D%92%E5%B9%B4%E6%97%85%E5%BA%97_%28%E4%B8%93%E8%BE%91%29>

### Lyric axis

The lyric axis is industrial city memory, family collapse, a father / mother / child structure, and the brutality of ordinary life under changing times. The title's violence should not be flattened into slogan or aesthetic shock.

### Music / arrangement axis

The arrangement carries more fire than the lyric alone suggests. Brass / band force and live-performance memory can turn defeat into collective ignition. This case checks whether the listener hears resistance, not only tragedy.

### Public / review-derived axis

Public sources place the song within Chinese independent rock influence and Shijiazhuang city discourse. That context is useful, but future songs must not inherit its city-specific objects by default.

### Processed positive comment patterns

- A useful comment connects the song to real changes in Shijiazhuang, while still hearing the band's force rather than only nostalgia.
- A useful comment ties the song to rock-youth memory, physical live scenes, pogo, travel for records, and the passage from youth fire to later solitary listening.
- A useful comment hears `kill` as tragic pressure but also as an urge to change life.

### Processed rejected comment patterns

- Beautiful aphorisms about ideal and desire are too generic if they reduce social observation and rock fire to a slogan.
- Pseudo-bohemian comments may use music as identity decoration rather than listening evidence.
- Social self-pity that hears only being trapped can miss the song's rock core.

### Processed user-ear notes

- The song is a classic image of the fire inside many rock listeners.
- After reality destroys fantasy, the song still tells life it will not surrender.
- Brass and orchestral force can ignite live crowds and turn reflection into physical fire.
- The song sees through fantasy but keeps idealism; it accepts reality without accepting fate.
- The arrangement already contains progressive-rock-like color, partly hidden behind the lyric's force.

### Final user judgment

`生活让我火大，我依然火大，燃烧着。`

### Non-transferable details

Do not transfer Shijiazhuang, pharmaceutical factories, malls, specific local institutions, pogo, or rock-youth biographies to every urban rock song.

### Portable diagnostic question

Does the comment hear only failure, or does it hear the song's remaining resistance?

## Case 5: 你要跳舞吗 / New Pants

### Song-first listening profile

`你要跳舞吗` is not just a happy dance song. It is a body-command song: repetitive, direct, disco / new-wave / rock-based, and suited to turning ordinary pressure into physical release. Public reference material identifies New Pants as a Chinese rock band associated with new wave, punk rock, disco, and pop rock.

Source lead: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Pants>

### Lyric axis

The lyric axis is intentionally simple: the repeated invitation matters less as poetry than as a command to let the body answer before language does.

### Music / arrangement axis

The music works through repetition, beat insistence, retro-dance texture, and crowd motion. The central question is not `is it happy?` but `what kind of body does it produce?`

### Public / review-derived axis

New Pants' public style context helps explain why this dance can be read through rock-youth bodies rather than only nightlife or generic fun. That does not make all dance songs resistance songs.

### Processed positive comment patterns

- A useful comment hears the song as dance for failed, tired, awkward, or over-contained people rather than shiny party people.
- A useful comment distinguishes rock-body release from nightclub entertainment.
- A useful comment treats rock as a substitute body: louder, braver, stranger, and more alive than the listener may feel in daily life.
- A useful comment reads the song as idealistic alliance and shared escape rather than romance.

### Processed rejected comment patterns

- Generic exam or life advice does not connect to this song.
- Using the song as study motivation may be personal use, but it is weak listening evidence.
- A lyric-name joke can be meme noise unless it changes the public listening field.

### Processed user-ear notes

- The song can belong to crowded Beijing commutes, small rented rooms, and a grey daily life where the soul still wants to dance.
- Live experience may turn individual movement into crowd-carried movement: the listener does not dance alone, the crowd dances them.
- It offers temporary amnesty from unfairness and reality: now is dance time.
- Repetition in lyric and composition becomes the spell that forces body motion.
- Its disco feeling is not nostalgic past; it belongs to the immediate present.

### Final user judgment

`摇滚青年的广场舞专曲，不是贬义，形容经典。`

### Non-transferable details

Do not transfer Beijing, Modern Sky proximity, commuting, rental rooms, crowd-surfing, or rock-youth identity to every dance song.

### Portable diagnostic question

What kind of body action does this song ask for: pleasure, release, survival, escape, social display, or resistance?

## Case 6: 最炫民族风 / Phoenix Legend

### Song-first listening profile

`最炫民族风` is not a song that needs to be rescued from vulgarity. Its power is mass bodily access: strong beat, simple positive lyric, recognizable hook, public dance use, and meme-reframed afterlife. Public reference material notes its 2012 online popularity, extensive parody / mashup circulation, and use as a square-dance track.

Source lead: <https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%80%E7%82%AB%E6%B0%91%E6%97%8F%E9%A3%8E>

### Lyric axis

The lyric axis is direct, bright, and public-facing rather than inwardly poetic. The words are built for participation more than private interpretation.

### Music / arrangement axis

The song relies on strong pulse, catchy repetition, bright instrumental color, and vocal division of labor. Its value lies in how easily different bodies can join it.

### Public / review-derived axis

The meme and square-dance afterlife are not merely noise. They are part of how the song became a public object across generations. However, not every viral or vulgar song gains this kind of public vitality.

### Processed positive comment patterns

- A useful comment reframes square-dance motion as a humorous but moving survival tactic against loneliness.
- A useful comment captures generational reversal: rejecting, understanding, becoming, and surpassing the parental taste position.
- A useful comment notices that years of changing listeners did not erase the song's public power.

### Processed rejected comment patterns

- Time-capsule jokes about old internet moments may be funny but weak if they do not reconnect to the song's body or public life.
- Family anecdotes are too thin if they only report children liking the track.
- Platform or copyright return comments are not listening evidence.

### Processed user-ear notes

- The song is close to a public recognition test: many people can enter by singing along.
- Its long-term repetition can reveal a vigorous mass social energy.
- It can turn bad reality into comic self-affirming movement.
- The vocal division foregrounds Linghua's carrying force, with Zeng Yi functioning more as support / rap accent in this track.
- Extreme earthiness can become collective elegance; extreme vulgarity can become public form.

### Final user judgment

`主流正能量，抖腿土嗨的节奏节拍，唤醒每个人的笑容。`

### Non-transferable details

Do not transfer square dance, parent-child taste reversal, national-pop optimism, or meme afterlife to every popular or vulgar song.

### Portable diagnostic question

Is the meme merely noise, or has it become part of the song's public listening body?

## How MSSL may use this document

- As a light audit guide for LLM listening reports.
- As a human-calibration seed set for comment-to-song alignment.
- As an example of song-first analysis before comment use.
- As a warning against treating lyrics, comments, or public memes as song truth.
- As a guide for deciding whether a comment is useful context, weak context, or noise.

## How MSSL must not use this document

- Do not classify all songs using these six cases.
- Do not generate final report prose from these cases directly.
- Do not assume future youth songs should resemble `七里香`.
- Do not assume future dance songs should resemble `你要跳舞吗`.
- Do not assume future popular meme songs should resemble `最炫民族风`.
- Do not use user-ear notes as universal truth.
- Do not store or reproduce raw source comments.

## Minimal rule

```text
Each song must first be heard as itself.
A comment is useful only when it reconnects to that song's own listening field.
The transferable unit is the diagnostic question, not the seed case answer.
```
